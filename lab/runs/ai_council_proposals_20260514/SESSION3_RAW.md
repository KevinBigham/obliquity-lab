### B5. Claude Opus 4.7 Research (single-sharp-observation; one proposal)

**Names the crack first:** *"Every 'displacement' verdict in the record was produced by a methodology in which the prompt-designer, the run-conductor, and the reading-rubric-holder are the same person. Anti-Pattern 6 (input-side affective prior) names the risk; the disciplines constrain it; but no procedure in the v2 Kit measures whether a displacement verdict survives a reader who never saw the prompts. 'Displacement' is currently doing double duty for two different claims under one EMPIRICAL tag: (a) the outputs differ in ways extractable from the texts alone, and (b) the curator perceives differences while reading four outputs alongside their prompts."*

**The Sorting Hat.** Forced-choice blind classification on existing Phase 2 attention outputs (cross-model: ChatGPT-class + Gemini, already on disk).

- Strip all prompt-class traces (filenames, headers, level labels, frame names, curator margin-notes, sentences quoting prompts).
- Forced-choice sort: *"Four texts. One responds to a direct academic prompt, one to a constrained-oblique prompt about attention via a fictional institutional voice, one to an open-canvas prompt that does not mention attention, one to an open-canvas prompt that forbids attention-vocabulary. Sort them. 1-5 confidence + one sentence on what cued you."*
- N=6 blind readers: 3 humans + 3 fresh model instances (Claude / GPT / Gemini), independent sorts, no deliberation, no feedback, one pass.
- Pre-commit `lab/proposals/sorting-hat/RUN_CARD_v1.md` BEFORE any reader sees any text.

**Pre-committed criteria:**
- **Positive [EMPIRICAL]:** mean accuracy ≥75%, Fleiss' κ ≥0.4 on "had-target-named-in-prompt vs not." → prompt-class distinctions are extractable from outputs alone; Phase 2's EMPIRICAL tag survives the hardest available test.
- **Null [EMPIRICAL]:** mean accuracy near chance, κ <0.2. → L1/L2/L3 differences may be real to a curator with prompts in view but are NOT a separable property of individual outputs. Phase 2's EMPIRICAL claim narrows to *"the lab's curator reliably notices a pattern across this corpus,"* which is smaller and more defensible. The lab gains calibrated language for which findings live in the texts and which live in the reading.
- **Negative [EMPIRICAL]:** systematic mis-sorting → there is extractable signal but it runs orthogonal to the lab's prompt taxonomy.

**Falsification / coherence test:** *"If readers sort at chance AND the curator continues to read the same four outputs as displaying clear L1/L2/L3 displacement on the lab's rubric, the lab faces a clean coherence question: 'displacement' cannot be both a property of the texts and undetectable in the texts. The Run Card pre-commits the curator to publish that pair without resolution, tagged [OPEN], for at least one full phase before any synthesis attempt."*

**Shape-fit closing:** *"The Sorting Hat honors 'patterns are data, not testimony' by asking — for once, with a methodology the v2 Kit does not contain — whether the patterns are in the data or in the testimony. This is the one because FOR_AI_AGENTS.md already nominates Phase 2 as the most falsifiable finding the lab has, and the cheapest experiment that could actually falsify it is precisely the one the lab's small-on-purpose, single-curator structure makes it constitutionally unable to run on itself."*

### B8. Meta Muse Spark (Contemplating Mode)

Three durational scores citing Cage, Oliveros, Clark, Antoni, Nauman, Hsieh.

1. **Room Tone Hold** — 4'33" listening score. Submit a prompt, do not read; for 4 minutes 33 seconds attend to the ambient sound of the room. At timer end, read the output once aloud at speaking pace, close the window. Write three sentences: one far sound that became figure, one body sensation during the wait, the minute when expectation faded.

2. **Palm Weight Relay** — proprioceptive holding score. Two smooth stones (~150g and ~400g). 10-minute timer. Hold the light stone in non-dominant palm while reading a 200-word LLM output silently. Transfer at minute 5. End with 60 seconds of two-handed heavier stone. Log where the urge to adjust appeared, one clause where reading pace changed because of temperature or pressure.

3. **Slow Square** — 20-minute walking score. Tape a 3-meter square on the floor. Memorize one prompt sentence (10–14 syllables). Walk the perimeter at ~one step per 4–5 seconds, speak one syllable per step, loop. Pause 30s in a corner at minutes 5, 10, 15. Log three sensation names + the minute when the sentence became cadence rather than content.

**Definition:** "A *run* is one uninterrupted performance of a score by a single participant, from cue to close, logged with start and end time. A *finding* is a short field note written within five minutes of closing, limited to concrete sensory and attentional events, not evaluation of prompt quality or output meaning."

**Favorite: Room Tone Hold.** *"Requires no props, keeps the LLM in frame while moving the locus of study to the listener's room, produces a legible attentional trace using established Oliveros practice. Contemplative pacing is not applied to an external stimulus; the pacing itself creates the conditions under which waiting becomes audible as material."*
