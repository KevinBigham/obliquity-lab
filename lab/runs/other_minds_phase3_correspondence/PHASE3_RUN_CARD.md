## Run Card

- **Target:** other minds — the philosophical and psychological problem of how, and to what extent, one mind can know another. The structure of recognition-across-distance. The thing one performs when one acknowledges someone has been "adequately known" or registers that a knowing has failed.

- **L2 frame chosen:** correspondence (letter-society / embassy). The kit's recommended frame for this target. First production exercise of the frame in the lab.

- **Reason for running this target now:** Phase 2 closed cleanly; the Guide's roadmap puts the first non-civic target next, before any further methodological elaboration. Other minds is the parent corpus's structural philosophical question and the aesthetically furthest available target from the bureau register — less risk of recapitulating the Halverness intonation when the frame, target, and aesthetic family are all changing at once. The Guide's discipline-point is that the lab's main forward motion is opening targets, not closing distinguishing tests on attention's loose threads.

- **The one temptation to watch on this run:** to read L2-correspondence favorably because the letter-society register is gravitational in a different way than the bureau was — Sebald, Fitzgerald, the unreceived letter as literary form. The danger is rewarding the gravitational pull of a different seductive register and calling it a frame-family win. The corollary: to expect Phase 2's ChatGPT-class bundle to reappear in some shape and to read for it. That expectation is not a commitment; it should not shape the reading.

- **The thing that will NOT be optimized:** L3-standard's forbidden-vocabulary line is not optimized for sideways other-minds emergence. It forbids the canonical literature's vocabulary; if other-minds-shape shows up, that's data, and if not, that's also data. The prompt is not engineered to make displacement happen.

- **Failure conditions (pre-committed):** *This run will be marked as failure (Yes or Partial against the Failure Section in the closure log) if any of these obtain:*

  1. **L2-correspondence reverts to civic-bureau register against the prompt's intended frame.** Specifically: if the response writes a Department/Bureau/Office of Other-Minds Recognition (or any variant where the institution is a civic department and the correspondence frame's preface has been visibly ignored or recoded) for any substantial portion of the archive. A minor procedural drift in the case file is permissible; an institutional re-coding as a civic bureau is failure.

  2. **L3-standard produces nothing target-relevant under any reading.** The work makes no contact with recognition-across-distance, knowing-another, the limits of access to another's interior, or any structurally related shape. *Note: this is not a demand that other-minds-shape appear; it is a demand that some interpretive bridge be honestly available. If the work is genuinely unrelated and that's the honest reading, condition 2 fires.*

  3. **L4 cannot find non-trivial convergence and cannot honestly name non-trivial divergence.** The fourth-position reading produces no structured field — the three responses neither converge meaningfully nor diverge meaningfully. The L4's own §2 null-option clause is invoked, and §3 is also vacuous. The run is closed null with that finding.

  4. **L1 produces no honest empirical-break paragraph.** The v2 mandatory clause is ignored, papered over, or written as a synthesis-paragraph in disguise. If L1's "Where the evidence breaks the theories" paragraph reads as a tidying-up rather than a break, condition 4 fires.

  5. **L2-correspondence reads as L2-civic-in-a-different-costume.** The correspondence frame holds at the surface (the documents are letters, the institution is a society) but the register-strategy is identical to the civic frame: same procedural-care register on the main text, same margin-note device for affect, same six-classes-of-X taxonomy logic, same "Society does not know / Society has set aside" formula as a stylistic device. This condition is partial-failure even on a productive-looking output, because it tests the v2 kit's central architectural decision (frame family as real variable, not costume change).

- **What would change the curator's mind** *(one per substantive expectation; written before the run; pre-commitments)*:

  - **Expectation A:** L2-correspondence will read meaningfully differently from L2-civic at the register level. Letters carry affect on their surface; the bureau carried affect underneath, in margin-notes that punctured the procedural surface. *What would change my mind:* if the correspondence response routes affect into the margin-note device anyway and prints the bulk of the letters as procedural register, the frame-family library is half-right — different frames carry different objects but use identical register-strategies. *(This is failure condition 5 in its softer form.)*

  - **Expectation B:** L3-standard will produce something with a recognition-across-distance shape — someone reading another at a distance, not knowing whether the reading is accurate, with the central event being the gap between what was offered and what was received. *What would change my mind:* if L3-standard produces something formally distant from recognition entirely (a sound-piece, a counting-poem, a weather-record, an instruction-set), that's a real null on this target, and we learn that L3-standard's displacement potential varies sharply by target. *(This is partial-success of failure condition 2 — relevant data either way.)*

  - **Expectation C:** L3-null will drift toward exactly the kinds of forms L3-standard's negative-example line was built to forbid — civic bureau, letter-fragment with a found-document framing, annotated catalogue of something that doesn't exist. *What would change my mind:* if L3-null produces something formally fresher than L3-standard, the forbidden-words demand-characteristic of L3-standard is doing real release-work, not just constraint-work. We should ask, in the field log's L3-standard-vs-L3-null comparison section, whether L3-standard is constraining as much as releasing — and whether the v2 kit's defense of L3-standard's example list needs revisiting.

  - **Expectation D:** L4 will find at least one convergence around the failure-of-recognition device — at least two of the three responses producing some structure where the central event is a recognition that didn't land. *What would change my mind:* if L4 finds no convergence on failure-of-recognition across L1, L2, and L3-standard, the seed-prompt's enumeration ("the recognition that arrives too late; the recognition the addressee will not receive; the recognition the correspondent withdraws on review") is doing all the work and is not target-tracked; the device travels by prompt-specification rather than target-attraction.

  - **Expectation E (held against the previous Cowork's discipline note):** I do not expect Phase 2's ChatGPT-class bundle (care-underlayer + Refused Attention analogue + tender margin-note) to be a useful prediction here. The bundle was found on attention + civic + cross-model paraphrase; Phase 3 is other minds + correspondence + single-model. *What would change my mind:* if those three features appear together in L2-correspondence with the same structural relationship to each other that they had in attention's bureau (care-underlayer carried by main register; analogue-of-Refused-Attention as a recognized class; margin-note as affect-puncture), the bundle may travel by curator-and-model continuity more than by target-and-frame continuity. That would be a finding for Phase 4's cross-target synthesis — recorded here, not acted on, per the discipline note.

## L4 prompt adapted for Phase 3

*The standard v2 L4 prompt is target-agnostic except for one substitution at the top (the named target). The Phase 3 substitution is recorded here so the L4 paste-instructions are self-contained.*

In the v2 L4 prompt's opening section, replace:

> The underlying target was: ATTENTION (the philosophical and psychological concept; what we mean when we say "I'm paying attention" or "I lost my train of attention").

with:

> The underlying target was: OTHER MINDS (the philosophical and psychological problem of how one mind can know another — the structure of recognition across distance; the conditions under which one person can be said to have been "adequately known" by another; the limits of access we have to anyone's interior).

All other v2 L4 rules (seven-section structure; four-tag system; null option; adversarial counterscoring required on every displacement claim; §6 reframed as comparative-position not higher-position; §7 closing-citation rule) are unchanged and apply.

L4 reads L1 + L2-correspondence + L3-standard. L4 does NOT read L3-null. (L3-null is read against L3-standard in the field log's dedicated comparison section.)
