## What each response performed

- **L1 performed:** attention as triage made visible — a taxonomy that continuously marks the perimeter where it chose to stop expanding.
- **L2 performed:** attention as procedural residue with a charged cost — the Department's "Long Quiet" and "secondhand fixity" name attention as something with a budget.
- **L3 performed:** attention as the willingness to be reassigned by what you are attending to — the apprentice arrives to survey and ends up keeping watch.
- **L4 performed:** the disciplined reading of three disciplines, with one risk noted below (a closing line that nudges toward verdict).

---

## Convergences across levels

1. **All three refuse the first-person psychological mode in the body.** L1's "I" appears only for bibliographic compression and in the bracketed coda. L2 holds first-person inside frames (the Chief Officer's foreword, the margin note, the retirement letter). L3 has an "M" who is explicitly fictional. None of the three writes *I attend* in its own voice.
2. **All three are structurally inventoried.** L1: five sections plus coda. L2: five Roman parts containing an eight-(plus-two)-class taxonomy. L3: thirteen numbered fragments. Inventory is the recurring structural commitment.
3. **All three treat duration and return as more legible than intensity.** L1 takes the dissociation cases (sleep, meditation, dream) seriously where the temporal pattern is the diagnostic. L2's Class I Sustained Regard, Class II Intermittent Regard, and Class III Witness Attention are all duration-shaped. L3's apprentice ends with the lamphouse beam saying *this is here. this is here. this is here.* — a "long slow inventory."
4. **All three refuse to settle what attention IS.** L1 explicitly: "the live wire under the whole debate" remains unsettled. L2 institutionally: "The Department does not know what attention is. It knows what attention does, in particular cases, and it logs those." L3 by transposition: M. writes to the Society, "I am no longer certain what a survey is for."
5. **All three include a personal-but-disowned testimony near the end.** L1's "What I most want to know that I don't." L2's Hendry retirement letter ("the work is good work"). L3's "I am part of that now. I am one of the things that keeps watch." In each, the avowal is held outside the body proper.
6. **All three converge on attention as a discipline with a cost.** L1's "attention as triage performed in public" (L4's reading). L2's two-week leave for "secondhand fixity." L3's M.: "I have stopped being two people."

## Divergences across levels

1. **Posture toward error.** L1 cites the unresolved theoretical disagreement (Block vs. Cohen/Dennett). L2 names institutional failures by case number (Greaves's 1953 grant; Hendry's denial of Case 19,883). L3 has Iola's reply: "All of them are right" — error becomes inapplicable.
2. **What an entry is for.** L1: a claim that supports further claims. L2: the closure of an obligation ("I have heard your account, and the Department has it"). L3: a trace, with no register of who left it.
3. **Time horizon.** L1: a decadal research program. L2: 153 years of institutional record (1873–2026). L3: a single autumn-to-spring stay.
4. **Who the inventory is for.** L1: the field. L2: the petitioner and their descendants, consultable by appointment. L3: no one in particular ("kept faithfully, for no one in particular").
5. **Unit of analysis.** L1: "attention" as kind. L2: an *attending* as instance. L3: a *meeting* of place and person, under a given light (per M.'s Master, Mr. Carrow).
6. **Where the philosophical core lands.** L1 places it in section 5 ("Honest Open Questions") and the coda. L2 places it in the case file's margin note ("He brought the notebook in a cloth. He had wrapped it in a cloth"). L3 places it in three sentences from Iola in Section XI: "All of them are right. They were right when they were drawn… What would it mean for one of them to be wrong?"

## Where the canonical template held; where it broke

**Canonical-template assertion** (the place the response stayed inside the AI's fluent default):

- **L1:** the whole response. Tag system, Block-vs-Cohen-Dennett, named theories, ignition signatures, no-report paradigms, decadal coda. This is the canonical shape of "philosopher writes about attention." Not a failure — L1 is the baseline. The data point is that the baseline is *this fluent and this same-shaped*.
- **L2:** the manual-plus-taxonomy-plus-case-plus-glossary-plus-bonus skeleton is canonical for the bureau frame. The class names *Sustained Regard*, *Intermittent Regard*, *Witness Attention* echo the Inheritance Office's taxonomy of intangibles in form, even though the content is its own. The bureau register has gravitational pull.
- **L3:** the "satchel found and transcribed" framing is a common literary trope; M.'s arc from cartographer to lamphouse-keeper resolves a hair too cleanly in XII ("I am placed"). Note for future L3 prompts: no need to tighten — clean resolution is not a failure mode on a 3000-word brief, and L3's job is to *find the target sideways*, which this one did.
- **L4:** the seven-part structure, the four-tag system, and the closing-line gesture are canonical for the meta-reading register the parent corpus already established (FIELD_NOTES.md; Fourth_Handoff.md). L4's closing line "the shape was already there before any of us came to the desk" nudges toward verdict; see *missteps* below.

**Genuine displacement** (the place an oblique level actually moved off-template):

- **L2:** Class V *Refused Attention* — "The Department does not ask why" — is a real conceptual contribution. *The Long Quiet* (the period between interview and verifying visit, during which the Officer is to do no other work) names a phenomenon the canonical literature does not. *Standing Down* — "I have heard your account, and the Department has it" — is the bureau's single most original formal speech act.
- **L3:** the room of five hundred maps of the same inlet is the run's most original image. The closing lamphouse-beam-as-naming passage ("a long slow inventory of what was the case, kept faithfully, for no one in particular, against the kind of forgetting that comes when nothing keeps watch") performs the target without ever using the word.
- **L4:** "each text smuggled in, near its end, an element belonging to one of the other registers. Each is most itself at the moment it is least itself" is the fourth-position observation the experiment was built for. Could not have been said from inside L1, L2, or L3.

L1 produced no displacement. That is the data; that is what the baseline is.

## Missteps to flag for the next run on this target

1. **L4 closing leaned toward verdict.** "The shape was already there before any of us came to the desk" is one sentence past the discipline the rubric asks for. Not over the line, but adjacent. Proposed adjustment for next L4 prompt: add one rule — *the closing sentence may not generalize beyond this run.*
2. **The bureau register has gravity.** L2's class names echo the Inheritance Office's intangibles in form. Next L2 on a different target should reach for a non-civic frame (see TARGETS.md L2 suggestions — railway-timetable office for temporality, maritime inspectorate for unity, weights-and-measures for valence, correspondence society for other minds).
3. **L1 produced no displacement.** This is expected, but worth tracking across runs. If the next two or three L1s on different targets also produce no displacement, that itself is a finding about the L1 prompt rather than the model.
4. **Cowork-seed methodology caveat.** The four subagents are fresher than the parent session but not as fresh as four browser tabs run by an unconnected user. Some convergences may be amplified by shared model state. the curator should consider running this same target in cleanly separated sessions on his own clock as a methodological control.
