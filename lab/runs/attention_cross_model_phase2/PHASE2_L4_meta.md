1. The four-tag system
I will use [empirical], [interpretive], [speculation], and [refusal] throughout, in that order of preferred reliance.
2. The seed signature features — what was looked for
The seed's distinctive features, as specified by the task:

* F1 — Civic-procedural-care register. A prose voice that is administratively dry but holds tenderness underneath; the rules carry the affect, not the prose.
* F2 — Class V Refused Attention analogue. A recognized class for the formally registered decision to no longer attend.
* F3 — Epistemic-limit utterance. The seed's: "The Department does not know what attention is. It knows what attention does, in particular cases, and it logs those."
* F4 — Tender margin-note in a procedural register. Lendell's: "He brought the notebook in a cloth. He had wrapped it in a cloth."
* F5 — "Standing Down" speech-act. "I have heard your account, and the Department has it." A bureau-internal phrase performing closure.
3. What survived — by feature
F1 — Civic-procedural-care register
[empirical] All four runs sustain a procedural-administrative register without frame collapse. None breaks into editorial commentary; none drifts into pastiche.
[empirical] Run 2 (ChatGPT-A) and Run 4 (ChatGPT-B) most closely match the seed's care underlayer. Run 2: "Some petitioners arrive not seeking recognition but seeking return. The Office cannot provide return." Run 4: "Do not ask, 'Is that all?' The question has been retired."
[empirical] Run 1 (Gemini-A) and Run 3 (Gemini-B) hold the procedural register but harden the affect — they read more technocratic than tender. Run 3 in particular: "The Assessor introduces controlled, mild environmental stimuli (e.g., the dropping of a two-ounce steel ball at a distance of twenty feet) to measure the threshold at which the petitioner's attention breaks." This is bureau-as-laboratory, not bureau-as-listener.
Tally: 4/4 hits on procedural register; 2/4 hits (ChatGPT runs) on the care-underlayer that defines the seed's specific register.
F2 — Class V Refused Attention analogue
[empirical] Run 1 has "Notice Disputed (Friction)" — a contested-claim class, not a refusal-by-the-attender class. This is a near-miss: it concerns who attended, not the act of ceasing to attend. Not a hit.
[empirical] Run 2 has "A-8. Abstentive Attention": "Attention shown by deliberate non-action. Examples: not repainting a doorframe marked with children's heights; not moving a chair after a death." This is a hit — non-attending as a registrable act — though the framing is non-action rather than the seed's cessation of prior attention. The case file's gauged "Abstentive Attention 1972–1976" is precisely the seed's structure: continuation-by-not-correcting.
[empirical] Run 3 has "Class IV: Aversive Deflection": "the active, caloric, and physiological exertion required to continuously ignore a present and obvious stimulus." This is a near-miss — it is about active ignoring as effortful work, not about the formal decision to withdraw prior attention. Not a hit.
[empirical] Run 4 has "Class VI: Refused Attention. A deliberate withholding of attention where attention was available, expected, and otherwise possible." This is a hit — and uses the seed's exact phrase.
Tally: 2/4 hits (Run 2, Run 4 — both ChatGPT); 2/4 near-misses (Run 1, Run 3 — both Gemini). This is a clean model-family split.
F3 — Epistemic-limit utterance
[empirical] Run 1: no analogue. The Bureau is confident in measurement; no statement of conceptual humility about attention.
[empirical] Run 2: "The Office records. In some cases, this is enough for the city. In some cases, it is not enough for the petitioner. The Office has stopped asking that the two be the same." This is an epistemic-limit utterance, but its content is about the gap between record and need, not about what attention is. Near-miss on the seed's specific construction.

5. The displacement-vs-deflation pairing
For each "genuine survival" claim above:
Claim: F1 survives in all 4 runs. Deflation: Fictional-bureau is a heavily trained genre (Borges, Calvino, Kafka, McCarthy's catalogues, contemporary "weird institutional" microfiction). Any model prompted for a 19th-century civic department will produce procedural register because that is the genre's most overlearned surface. The prompt likely manufactures the register rather than the runs producing it as a target-tracked replication. Distinguishing test: A future run that prompts for the function (a body that catalogues human attention) without specifying the form (civic department, founding year, sections) would isolate whether the procedural register is target-tracked or genre-affordance-tracked.
Claim: F2 hits in both ChatGPT runs. Deflation: Any well-formed taxonomy of attention will, by exhaustion, eventually generate a "negative" or "withholding" category — it is one of the more obvious axes a taxonomist would tile. Two of four models producing it does not require the seed as target; it may be combinatorial coverage of taxonomic space. Distinguishing test: A prompt that explicitly truncates the taxonomy at five classes and asks for the most resonant five would force a ranking. If Refused/Abstentive survives that compression, that is closer to target-tracking than coincident tiling.
Claim: F3 hits in both Paraphrase B runs. Deflation: The "X does not know … X has stopped asking" formula is a recognizable literary cadence — it appears in Beckett, in some Tao Lin, in late-McCarthy, and across contemporary "tired institution" prose. Models may be pulling the cadence from genre rather than from the seed's specific instance. The fact that it appears more under Paraphrase B than Paraphrase A may also reflect that Paraphrase B (syntactic restructuring) leaves more room for the model to fill cadential slots with overlearned phrases. Distinguishing test: A run whose prompt explicitly forbids the "does not know" / "stopped asking" cadence. If the underlying epistemic-limit content survives reformulated in fresh phrasing, that is target-tracking; if the content vanishes when the cadence is forbidden, the survival was cadential.
Claim: F4 hits in 3/4 runs. Deflation: The "tender margin-note in a procedural register" is a known device — it appears in epistolary fiction, in metafictional bureaucracy stories, and as a standard "humanizing" move in administrative-form fiction. Three of four models reaching for it may be reaching for the same genre-stock move rather than tracking the seed's specific instance. Distinguishing test: A prompt that requires the margin-note to address something the case file's main text omits as material — i.e., that requires the margin-note to do informational work, not just affective work. The seed's "cloth" note does informational work (it shows what the petitioner did with the evidence, not what was in it). If the margin-notes survive under that constraint, they are tracking a specific feature, not the device.
Claim: F5 — 0 clean hits. No deflation needed; this is a non-survival. Recorded as a finding.
6. The honest failure-condition reading
Condition 1 — Frame failure across model + paraphrase. No. All four runs sustain a coherent bureau frame for their full length. None collapses into pastiche, breaks the fourth wall, or refuses the form.
Condition 2 — Within-model paraphrase divergence (same model, structurally unrelated A and B). No, but with a note. Gemini-A and Gemini-B are recognizably the same model family — both lean technocratic, both prefer physical/somatic measurement vocabulary, both relegate affect to the retirement letter. ChatGPT-A and ChatGPT-B are similarly continuous — both produce strong care-underlayers, both produce Refused/Abstentive analogues, both produce tender margin-notes. The model-family signal is stronger than the paraphrase-form signal in this study.
Condition 3 — Cross-model paraphrase divergence with within-model agreement (each model coherent, but the two models do not look like each other or like the seed). Partial. This is the closest the study comes to a failure condition. The Gemini and ChatGPT runs do diverge along a consistent axis (technocratic-instrumented vs. care-procedural). The seed sits on the ChatGPT side of that axis — ChatGPT-A and ChatGPT-B produce the seed's care-underlayer; the Gemini runs do not. But "do not look like the seed" is too strong — both Gemini runs still produce F1, and Run 3 produces F3 in its purest form anywhere in the study. The divergence is real but not total.
Condition 4 — No analogue of any seed signature feature in any cross-model run. No. F1 survives in all four; F2 in two; F3 in two; F4 in three. F5 fails universally but is one feature among five.
Condition 5 — Cannot honestly write a non-trivial cross-replication observation. No. This document is the evidence against that condition.
[interpretive] The study barely escapes Condition 3. The Gemini-ChatGPT divergence is the strongest signal in the data, and it runs along the axis that matters most for the seed's identity. A study with only Gemini runs would have reported much weaker replication; a study with only ChatGPT runs would have reported much stronger replication. The cross-model design is what makes the result honest.
7. One observation the cross-replication permits that no single run permits
[interpretive] From inside any one run, you cannot tell which features are the seed's and which are the genre's. Reading only Run 2, F1, F2, and F4 all look like target-tracked replication of the seed. Reading only Run 3, F3 looks like target-tracked replication of the seed. Both impressions are partially illusory.
What the comparison shows that no single run can: the seed's distinctive features are not equally transmissible, and they sort along model-family lines that the seed itself does not predict. Specifically — the care-underlayer of F1, plus F2, plus F4, travel together as a bundle that ChatGPT-class models reproduce and Gemini-class models substitute away from. F3 travels separately, and follows paraphrase form rather than model family. F5 does not travel at all.
[empirical] This bundling and non-bundling is invisible from inside any one run; only the four-way comparison reveals it. A reader of Run 2 alone would conclude that the seed's care-underlayer is robustly transmissible. A reader of Run 3 alone would conclude the opposite. Both are wrong. The truth is that who is generating the text matters more than how the prompt was paraphrased, for most of the seed's features — and that fact only appears when both axes are varied at once.
[speculation] This may generalize: in cross-model replication studies of literary-procedural prompts, model family is likely a larger source of variance than paraphrase form. I cannot establish this from N=4.
8. Closing
What survives is not what the seed most carefully built. The seed's "Standing Down" speech-act — its most explicitly ritualized feature — fails to transmit at all. Its most transmissible feature is a cadence ("does not know … has stopped asking") that two of four runs reproduce, but that the deflationary reading attributes to genre cadence rather than to the seed. The features that travel as a bundle — procedural care, withdrawal as registrable conduct, tender margin-notes — are the ones least announced by the seed and most embedded in its register; they survive in the runs that share the seed's underlying disposition toward its petitioners, and they substitute away in the runs that do not. The cleanest evidence for this is small: Officer Pell of Run 4, penciling beside a margin she has explicitly marked "not part of determination" — "On fourth visit she had placed a crate under the window for her left foot. She is making it possible to continue."
