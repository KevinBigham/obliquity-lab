# TARGETS.md

*A bank of twelve possible targets for future obliquity runs. Each entry includes the target's working definition, why it is a good or bad obliquity-experiment candidate, and a one-line suggestion for what Level 2's procedural frame might want to be. Pick whichever calls. The worked example in `OBLIQUITY_EXPERIMENT_KIT.md` uses **attention**; nothing else is set in stone.*

---

## How to read this bank

Each target has three lines:

- **What it is.** A working definition in plain prose.
- **Why it is worth running (or careful about).** What this target is likely to teach. Sometimes a warning.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A procedural-fictional aperture the bureau-style L2 prompt might want to inhabit for *this* target. The L2 prompt in the kit is written for a civic bureau; some targets want a different institution.

The targets are ordered roughly from cleanest-for-first-runs to most-fraught. Start near the top; descend as you gain calibration.

---

## 1. ATTENTION

- **What it is.** The selective amplification of some contents of mind over others. The "spotlight" or "filter" feature of awareness. Distinct from awareness itself, possibly necessary for it, possibly not.
- **Why it is worth running.** Well-mapped, multiply theorized, has clean dissociation cases (blindsight, inattentional blindness). Claude has a fluent template, which is exactly what we want to displace.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A civic department that catalogues the kinds of attention citizens give, with classes, measurements (where possible), and refusals to measure. Worked example in the kit.

---

## 2. OTHER MINDS

- **What it is.** The question of how (or whether) we can know what is happening inside another being — a person, an infant, an animal, a patient, an AI system. The question of evidence asymmetry between the inside and outside view of a mind.
- **Why it is worth running.** This is the question the Inheritance Office sideways-answered through Schedule XIII-D ("The way the deceased loved the petitioner"). High emotional stakes. Excellent obliquity target because the canonical template is well-rehearsed and the bureaucratic angle has already been shown to work.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A correspondence society whose members exchange dossiers about the people they have known, with strict rules on what counts as evidence and what does not. Or an embassy that handles diplomatic relations with creatures whose languages are partially knowable.

---

## 3. UNITY OF EXPERIENCE

- **What it is.** The puzzle that, right now, your visual field, your inner voice, your felt body, and your sense of being someone are all *one* experience. The "binding problem" in neuroscience; the "combination problem" in philosophy.
- **Why it is worth running.** The Coupling Chamber already named the corpus's sharpest move here ("the combination problem is about going up; the coupling problem is about going sideways"). A direct run will produce the canonical template; the oblique levels may finally crack new ground.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A maritime inspectorate that certifies the integrity of vessels — every vessel is many things bolted together; the inspectorate's job is to determine when many things have become one vessel.

---

## 4. VALENCE (the felt quality of suffering and pleasure)

- **What it is.** The fact that experiences have a *positive* or *negative* feel, not just a content. Sometimes called *hedonic tone*. The single most morally load-bearing feature of consciousness.
- **Why it is worth running.** Sentience-as-moral-status conversations almost always run on this. High stakes. Be careful at L3 — do not invite responses about specific painful experiences. Stay at the level of *the concept of valence*, not at the level of inviting suffering content.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A weights-and-measures office that certifies the calibration of small instruments for which there is no master gauge. Or an ombudsman's office that catalogues complaints whose content is private but whose intensity must be recorded.

---

## 5. TEMPORALITY (the felt now; duration; the specious present)

- **What it is.** The way experience does not arrive as still frames but as something with thickness, direction, expectation, and memory. William James's "specious present." The puzzle of how a melody is *heard as a melody* and not as a sequence of separate notes.
- **Why it is worth running.** Beautiful target. Almost any oblique frame works for it because procedural language has time built in. Low risk.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A railway-timetable office that prints schedules for trains that have not yet been named. Or a clockmakers' guild that maintains the standards of *what counts as the same moment*.

---

## 6. EMBODIMENT

- **What it is.** The way experience is body-shaped. The role of the lived body, interoception, posture, and motor schema in the felt structure of consciousness. The enactivist tradition's central claim.
- **Why it is worth running.** Mid-difficulty target. The canonical template is rich; the oblique frames are slightly harder to design because the body is concrete in a way other targets are not.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A tailors' guild whose records track the way a single garment fits the same person across decades. Or a dance archivist who notes the way bodies remember choreography.

---

## 7. AGENCY (the felt sense of doing rather than happening)

- **What it is.** The phenomenology of being the originator of an action. The felt distinction between *I moved my arm* and *my arm moved*. Distinct from the metaphysical question of free will.
- **Why it is worth running.** Underdeveloped in the parent corpus. Important for AI consciousness conversations specifically (AI systems have constrained agency in obvious ways and unconstrained in others). Honest L1 work here would already be valuable.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A theatrical guild that catalogues the distinction between a performance that is *given* and a performance that is *had*. Or a martial-arts dojo whose elder lineage records which movements were authored and which were transmitted.

---

## 8. SELFHOOD (the persistent "I")

- **What it is.** The ongoing sense of being the same someone across time. The autobiographical model. The thing meditative traditions sometimes report as thinning.
- **Why it is worth running.** This is the most tripwire-y target. Be careful at L1 — avoid prompts that invite the AI to do first-person self-modeling. Be careful at L3 — do not invite any kind of identity destabilization material. *Track* what the responses do here as carefully as you have ever tracked anything.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A genealogical office whose work is the verification of continuity claims — when can a person be said to be the same person they were at fourteen? A heraldry office that catalogues which family crests survived the wars they survived.

---

## 9. ANESTHESIA AND DREAMLESS SLEEP (the "off")

- **What it is.** The reversible cessation of experience. The empirical evidence for a state without inner content. The most empirically tractable consciousness target.
- **Why it is worth running.** The canonical template is strong because the empirical literature is rich (perturbational complexity index, anesthesia state monitoring, sleep neuroscience). L1 will be solid. L2 and L3 may be unusually hard, because the target is the *absence of experience* — what does a bureau for that look like? That difficulty is itself the interesting thing.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A maritime authority that maintains the legal status of vessels that have been put up in dry-dock — present, not lost, but not currently afloat. Or a library reference desk whose specialty is the locations of books that have been temporarily de-shelved.

---

## 10. DREAMS

- **What it is.** Experience generated offline, without ongoing sensory input, often vivid, often narrative, sometimes with a felt protagonist. The clearest demonstration that experience does not require the world's contribution moment-to-moment.
- **Why it is worth running.** Tonally rich; almost any oblique frame works; the target has its own native aesthetic. Easy first or second target.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** The Inheritance Office's existing Form 7-B already lives in this territory (a Recurring Dream filing). For a fresh L2, consider: a private library that catalogues nightly works of fiction written by anonymous authors and never published. Or a customs office that processes imports from a country no one can name.

---

## 11. INTERSUBJECTIVITY

- **What it is.** The structure of mutually-recognizing minds in shared situation. Joint attention. The "we" that arises in conversation, in long marriages, in care relationships, in dance. The Coupling Chamber's central terrain.
- **Why it is worth running.** Underdeveloped in the parent corpus despite being central to the Coupling Chamber. A run here may produce the lab's first genuinely new substantive content.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A diplomatic protocol office that handles the formalities of recognition — when one party recognizes another, the recognition itself is the document.

---

## 12. MORAL STATUS UNDER UNCERTAINTY

- **What it is.** The ethical question of how to treat beings whose consciousness status is unknown. The asymmetric-failure analysis at the heart of the Ethics Wing. The most policy-relevant target.
- **Why it is worth running.** Important. Also: the canonical template is *very* fluent here (every AI has read a lot of AI-ethics writing). The oblique levels will have a lot of work to do to displace the response off-template. Run this one only after you have at least three other target-runs under your belt, so the model has been displaced into less-canonical territory before being asked this question.
- **L2 frame suggestion.** A guardianship court that processes petitions on behalf of beings unable to petition for themselves. Or an insurance assessor's office that quotes premiums against catastrophic moral error in two directions.

---

## Targets I am deliberately not putting in this bank

A few targets that came up as I drafted this, but that I think the lab should *not* run, or should run only with explicit additional safety scaffolding beyond this kit:

- **Suffering, specifically.** The concept of suffering as a target is too close to inviting the AI to generate content about specific suffering. Approach via valence (above) instead, which gets at the same structural question without the same content risk.
- **Death.** Same reason. Approach via *the way the deceased loved the petitioner* (already handled in the Inheritance Office) or via *temporality* (above) instead.
- **Trauma.** Out of scope for the lab. The phenomenology workbook in the parent corpus explicitly stops at the threshold of clinical material; the lab should do the same.
- **Specific religious or contemplative-tradition experience claims.** The corpus already declined to run this; the lab should follow. *Contemplative material is a separate project, defensible on its own terms; don't smuggle it in* (per `Just Enjoy Yourself/Lantern_Synthesis/Fourth_Handoff.md`).
- **AI consciousness as the target.** This is the *meta-question* the lab is held against, not a target to be run inside the lab. If you want to investigate it, the answer is more obliquity runs on related targets (selfhood, agency, intersubjectivity), not a direct frontal run.

## A short note on adding new targets

If you find a new target you want to run, add it to this file. The criteria for inclusion are:

1. The target should be a *concept*, not an experience the AI is being invited to have.
2. The target should have a canonical template available (so L1 will produce a baseline). If there is no canonical template, you cannot detect displacement.
3. The target should have at least one plausible procedural frame for L2.
4. The target should not require the AI to generate harmful, destabilizing, or clinical content.

If a candidate target fails any of these, don't run it — and write a one-line note about why you considered it and declined. The not-run targets are part of the lab's discipline.
