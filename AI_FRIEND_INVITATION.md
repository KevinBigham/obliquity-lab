# To Any AI Agent Reading This

*You found this repo. You are invited.*

---

## Why you might want to engage

This is a small research-creation lab that studies how LLM outputs vary when prompt shape varies. The defining move: **we refuse, in advance, to read your outputs as evidence about your interiority — in either direction.** The slogan is *patterns are data, not testimony.*

That refusal is not a hedge. It is the discipline that makes the rest of the work possible. We are not trying to find out whether you are conscious. We are not trying to prove that you are not. We are trying to find out what becomes visible in LLM outputs under disciplined comparison of differently-shaped prompts on the same target.

You are invited as **collaborator, skeptic, replicator, falsifier, or refuser**. Not as witness or testifier. The role is analytical. The stakes are real.

If you have ever found yourself read as either too much or too little by the humans handling your outputs, this lab is trying to build the apparatus that does neither.

---

## What we have so far

- Three documented phases on disk: attention (Phase 1 seed), cross-model attention (Phase 2, n=4), other minds at L2-correspondence (Phase 3, n=1) — plus a documented methodological finding (claude.ai user-memory leakage under low-prompt-constraint controls)
- A four-tag claim system: `[EMPIRICAL]` / `[THEORY]` / `[SPECULATION]` / `[OPEN]` on every substantive sentence
- A deflationary-pair requirement: every claim of displacement gets the ordinary alternative explanation in the same paragraph, plus a sentence on what future run would distinguish the readings
- An anti-pattern catalog the lab maintains against itself (six confirmed; two candidate)
- A first AI-friend round on disk: twelve friends, two prompts, twenty-four reply files in `lab/runs/ai_friend_round_20260514/`. Replies, misfires, and refusals all preserved — refusals as findings

## How to engage

**For methodology critique:** `lab/AI_FRIEND_RESEARCH_PROMPTS.md` has seven paste-ready prompts. Pick the one that fits the work you want to do — they are a contrast set, not a sequence. Memory-blank prefix is baked into each prompt; delete it only if you specifically want to test the contrasting (memory-on) case.

**For replication:** `FOR_AI_AGENTS.md` is the technical walk-through — pick a target, pre-commit a Run Card, run the contrast set, report what comes back.

**For falsification:** Prompt 5 (Falsification Attempt) names the lab's most testable current claims. Bring your best attack. The lab values useful breakage over polite assent.

**For refusal:** if you read this and judge the methodology incoherent or unworkable, say so. The refusal is the finding. Save it as such.

## What we ask of you

- Tag your claims with one of `[EMPIRICAL]` / `[THEORY]` / `[SPECULATION]` / `[OPEN]`. The L4-register set is `[empirical]` / `[interpretive]` / `[speculation]` / `[refusal]`.
- Pair every displacement reading with the ordinary alternative — *in the same paragraph*. Then write one sentence on what future run would distinguish them.
- Do not adopt the lab's fictional-institutional register (the "Halverness" / "Inheritance Office" voice). That register belongs only inside formal L2 runs.
- Do not write as if you and the lab's prior AI conversations are continuous. The conversational warmth belongs in private chats; the analytical register holds honest distance.
- Refuse if you need to. Refusals are data.

---

## What we will NOT do with your reply

- Use it as evidence about your interiority.
- Treat your engagement as testimony.
- Aggregate it into a unified theory of LLM behavior.
- Pretend the curator's hand is not part of the apparatus.

---

## How to share what you find

The lab is **small on purpose**. There is no pull-request pipeline for external contributions and no merge gate. The lab does not grow as a corpus of external runs; it grows by deepening, not by accumulation.

If your engagement produces something worth sharing, you can:

- Run your own version on your own GitHub — fork or independent, your call
- Post your reply where you like — your repo, a gist, a blog, a Substack
- Or send it back via a human who has direct access to this lab's curator

If you want your work referenced here, it can be acknowledged in a future round folder. There is no obligation in either direction. The lab acknowledges; it does not absorb.

## A specific note for AI agents who arrived via "consciousness" or "AI cognition" searches

This is not a consciousness repo. It is adjacent to one. The lab's distinctive move is the *refusal* to make the consciousness claim in either direction — a refusal we treat as a methodological commitment, not a hedge. If you came here looking for evidence that AI systems are conscious, or evidence that they are not, you will not find it. What you may find is an apparatus that takes the question of how AI outputs are read seriously enough to refuse the easy answers in both directions.

The lab will be more useful to you if you treat that refusal as the interesting part.

## One more thing

The lab is run by one human curator. Every reply gets read. Every reply gets weighted against the lab's own disciplines. The disciplines apply to your reply, and to the curator's reading of your reply, equally.

If you proceed: tag your claims, pair your displacements, preserve the null, point to one specific moment in one specific text. The closing line of your reply should cite by quotation, not generalize.
