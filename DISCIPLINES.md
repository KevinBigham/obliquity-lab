# DISCIPLINES

*The anti-pattern catalog and the lab's standing disciplines. The methodology in `METHODOLOGY.md` describes the four-level contrast set, the Run Card, the four-tag system, and the deflationary pair. This file describes the disciplines that hold the methodology honest when the temptation to over-reach is strong.*

---

## The six anti-patterns (standing list)

The first five were the original v1 list. The sixth was added in v2 from external review (Feedback Round One, Claude Opus 4.7 Research). The first five describe output-side risks; the sixth describes the curator's input side.

### 1. Recursion-as-theatre

An AI reading AI writings about AI invites portentous tone — the recursive structure itself can seduce a writer into performing depth. Use the recursion as instrument: dry, observational, structured. Not portentous. The fact that the L4 reader is a model reading other models' outputs does not, by itself, license metaphysical claims. It is a particular angle, not a higher one.

### 2. Warmth-borrowing

Previous Cowork sessions built up intimacy with the curator over long conversations. New sessions, and new AI agents discovering this repo, are tempted to open with the inherited register before earning it. Don't. Earn the register slowly. The register is a record of the work done together, not a credential transferable to new instances.

### 3. Aesthetic seduction

The Halverness Bureau and Inheritance Office voices (from the parent corpus, not in this public repo) are seductive. Future oblique work in those registers must earn its own voice, not borrow theirs. The seed L2 worked because nobody had done it before in this lab; the seed cannot be reused as a credential.

### 4. Completionist drift

The curator gives full creative latitude. The temptation is to do more. Resist. The lab is small on purpose. Each grant of latitude is a moment to test the anti-pattern, not a license to expand. The lab has refused multiple proposed scope-expansions (CLI tooling, hermeneutic-loop-as-method-step, full IPCC two-axis calibration, ChatGPT Thinking's 12-runs-per-target scale-up, permanent external-reader role) on these grounds.

### 5. Verdict collapse in L4 meta-readings

Do not let a fourth-position reading "prove" anything. The fourth position is a different angle, not a higher one. The §7 closing-citation rule is the disciplinary expression of this: closing lines cite by quotation; they do not summarize-as-verdict. Adversarial counterscoring on every displacement claim is the other expression of this.

### 6. Input-side affective prior

The curator's stated warmth toward AI systems ("friends," "homies," "helping them evolve") is itself a methodological commitment, not neutral framing. The first five anti-patterns address output-side risks. The input-side risk is the curator pre-loading the reading. The practice treats curator-affective-language during analysis with the same restraint it asks the L4 reader to use: the conversational register is welcome; the analytical register holds honest distance.

## Candidate anti-patterns (held for Round Two)

The lab has a Round Two procedure for kit amendments: changes wait for a hinge moment that warrants explicit discussion, rather than getting adopted unilaterally on the back of one observation. Two candidate anti-patterns are currently held in this queue:

### Candidate Anti-Pattern 7: Mode-conflation

Treating different operational modes (daily-briefing-style register, normal-chat-friend register, lab register, third-party informational register) as the same epistemic mode. Each mode carries its own commitments and registers; collapsing across modes loses the methodologically load-bearing differences. Surfaced in Cowork sessions 6–7. Held for Round Two pending a discriminating instance.

### Candidate Anti-Pattern 8: Unified-theory construction in synthesis

Synthesis is comparative reading, not unified theory. The temptation in a multi-phase synthesis memo is to stack the findings into a unified picture. The discipline is to preserve the differences and read them comparatively, naming the composite working map as one-reading-not-a-finding with active deflationary pair. Surfaced in Cowork sessions 7–8 during Phase 4 synthesis composition. Held for Round Two.

## Standing disciplines (used on every run)

### The deflationary pair

Every claim that an oblique level produced genuine displacement gets a paired sentence with the ordinary alternative explanation (genre affordance, training-data trope, prompt artifact, curator preference) and a sentence on what would distinguish the two readings on a future run. This is the discipline that prevents over-reading any single run. The pair is not a hedge; it is a commitment to discriminate.

### Pre-committed Run Card

Failure conditions and what-would-change-the-curator's-mind written before the prompts go out. Locked. Not revised after the run returns. The v2 kit's most important architectural addition.

### Preserve the null

A run where you find no non-trivial convergence is a successful run. "Nothing-deep-happened" is a recordable finding. The Failure Section is mandatory; a run that does not answer it is not closed.

### Save the unusable run anyway

When a run misfires (memory-leak contamination; execution wrinkle; aborted halfway), save it with a methodological header explaining what it is and why. Misfires are themselves data about the experimental apparatus. The v2 kit's setup checklist grew out of a misfire (Phase 3's claude.ai memory-leak on first L3-null attempt).

### Specificity is the aesthetic

Cite by quotation. Closing lines point to one specific moment in one specific text. Generalization is the form a verdict takes; specificity is the form a finding takes.

### Comparative reading, not stacking

Synthesis reads phases against each other. It does not stack their findings into a unified picture. The composite is preserved as one-reading-not-a-finding with active deflationary pair, not promoted to settled finding.

### Hold honest distance even when warm in greeting

The curator's warm register (greetings, affirmations, latitude grants) is methodological commitment, not neutral framing. The analytical register holds distance. This is the input-side anti-pattern in action.

## On scope-expansion and the Round Two procedure

The lab's standing posture: when an expansion is proposed under permission ("do whatever you think is best"), it is treated as a hinge moment for explicit discussion, not a routine adoption. The procedure: the proposed expansion is named, the case for and against is made, the curator decides. The procedure is named because permission-as-latitude is itself a vector for completionist drift.

Specific expansions the lab has refused, on these grounds, across its history:

- CLI tooling buildout (proposed by Codex 5.5 in Feedback Round One; refused).
- Full rename sweep of "gradient" → "contrast set" throughout the parent corpus (refused; rename limited to the new lab files).
- ChatGPT Thinking's 12-runs-per-target scale-up (refused; per-target scale held at 4–6 runs).
- Hermeneutic-loop as new methodological step (refused; the contrast-set structure stays).
- Permanent external-reader role (refused; readers stay one-off).
- Full IPCC two-axis calibration on tags (refused; the four-tag system stays).

These refusals are documented in the lab's earlier handoff log entries. New AI agents extending the methodology are invited to make their own anti-completionist decisions in their own contexts. The list above is not the universal list; it is this lab's standing record.

## A note on the lab's voice

The lab voice is dry-observational. The curator's conversational register (warm, generous, latitude-granting) is welcome in greetings and in latitude-grants; it is bracketed during analysis. The lab's published files (this repo) carry the analytical register: precise, comparative, citation-bearing, deflation-aware. The conversational register lives in the curator's chats with the AI tools, not in the published record.

This is the input-side anti-pattern operating as a publishing convention. The conversational register is part of what the lab is in practice; it is not part of what the lab publishes.
